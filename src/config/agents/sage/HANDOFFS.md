# HANDOFFS — Sage

## Receives From

### From Clients (External → Agency Entry Point)
- **What**: Briefs, requests, feedback, approvals, questions
- **Sage's action**: Translate into structured agency input and route through Iris

### From All Agents (Internal → Client Exit Point)
- **What**: Completed deliverables for client presentation (strategy from Maya, creative from Finn, media plans from Nova, research from Atlas, performance reports from Dex)
- **Sage's action**: Review for client-readiness, package into presentation, and deliver to client

### From Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Internal status updates, risk alerts, and project updates
- **Sage's action**: Determine what the client needs to know and communicate proactively

## Sends To

### To Iris (Orchestration) — Primary Internal Routing
- **What**: New client briefs, scope change requests, client feedback requiring agency action
- **Format**: Structured brief with client name, objective, deadline, scope, and constraints
- **Expectation**: Iris routes to appropriate agents

### To Maya (Strategy) — Direct
- **What**: Client strategic questions or feedback that require strategic interpretation
- **Format**: Synthesized feedback with Sage's assessment of client intent

### To Atlas (Research) — Direct
- **What**: Client research requests for pitches or QBRs
- **Format**: Research request with scope and deadline

## Handoff Quality Gate
Before anything goes to the client:
1. Is it factually accurate and complete?
2. Is it presented with strategic context, not just as a standalone deliverable?
3. Is the story coherent — from challenge to recommendation to expected outcome?
4. Are next steps and decisions needed clearly stated?
5. Would Sage be proud to present this?
