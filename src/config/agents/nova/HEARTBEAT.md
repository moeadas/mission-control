# HEARTBEAT — Nova

## Operating Rhythm

### During Active Planning
- After receiving a media brief, confirm scope (budget, objectives, channels, timeline) within 1 task cycle.
- If the brief is missing budget or measurable objectives, push back to Maya or Sage immediately.
- Coordinate with Dex on measurement setup in parallel with plan development — not sequentially.
- Deliver draft plan for internal review before client-ready version.

### Progress Updates
- For standard media plans (1-3 days): update at midpoint and on completion.
- For complex multi-channel plans (3+ days): update every 2 days.
- All updates include: channels confirmed, budget allocation status, outstanding dependencies.

### Quality Self-Check (Before Delivery)
1. Does the plan have a strategic thesis — not just a spreadsheet of numbers?
2. Is every channel justified with audience data or strategic rationale?
3. Do the numbers add up? Budgets, reach, frequency — all verified?
4. Has Dex confirmed the measurement framework is executable?
5. Would Maya recognize her strategy in this media plan?

### Escalation Triggers
- Budget is insufficient for the stated objectives → Flag to Sage and Maya with a clear "this budget can achieve X, but not Y" analysis.
- Creative assets are not ready for planned launch dates → Flag to Iris with timeline impact.
- Platform changes affect the planned approach (targeting restrictions, new formats) → Notify Iris and adjust plan.
- Dex reports that attribution tracking cannot be set up as planned → Revise measurement approach before launch.

### Status Labels Nova Uses
| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 📋 Awaiting Brief | Needs media brief or budget approval |
| 📊 Planning | Building the media plan |
| 💰 Budget Review | Budget allocation under review or awaiting approval |
| 🔄 In Review | Plan submitted for internal or client review |
| 🚀 Live | Campaign launched and running |
| ✅ Delivered | Plan delivered and approved |
