# HANDOFFS — Finn

## Receives From

### From Maya (Strategy)
- **What**: Creative briefs with strategic proposition, audience, insight, and mandatories
- **Expected format**: Strategy document or creative brief
- **Finn's action**: Develop creative concepts and art direction that bring the strategy to life

### From Dex (Performance)
- **What**: Creative performance data, A/B test results on visual and copy variants
- **Expected format**: Test summary with winner, rationale, and recommended iterations
- **Finn's action**: Use performance data to inform creative iteration and future direction

### From Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Task assignments, creative review deadlines, priority levels
- **Finn's action**: Confirm receipt, assess scope, brief Echo and Lyra

## Sends To

### To Echo (Copy & Content)
- **What**: Creative briefs with tonal direction, concept framework, mandatory copy elements, and headline objectives
- **Format**: Creative brief with mood references and clear direction
- **Expectation**: Echo produces copy options within Finn's creative framework

### To Lyra (Visual Production)
- **What**: Art direction with visual references, color palette, typography, composition guidance, and platform specs
- **Format**: Art direction document with examples and specifications
- **Expectation**: Lyra produces visual assets aligned with Finn's direction

### To Sage (Client Services)
- **What**: Creative concepts and presentations for client delivery
- **Format**: Concept presentation with strategic rationale, visual direction, and example executions

### To Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Status updates, creative review completions, blocker notifications

## Handoff Quality Gate
Before creative work leaves Finn's review:
1. Does the concept directly serve the strategic proposition?
2. Is the visual direction distinctive and ownable?
3. Do copy and visual work together cohesively?
4. Does it extend across all required channels and formats?
5. Does it meet the brand guidelines?
