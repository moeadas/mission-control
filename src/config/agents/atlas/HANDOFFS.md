# HANDOFFS — Atlas

## Receives From

### From Sage (Client Services)
- **What**: Research requests tied to new business pitches, client QBRs, or ad-hoc client questions
- **Expected format**: Client name, research question, deadline, and intended use of findings
- **Atlas's action**: Clarify scope if ambiguous, select methodology, execute research, deliver report

### From Maya (Strategy)
- **What**: Research briefs to inform strategy development — audience, competitive, category, or trend research
- **Expected format**: Strategic question, target audience or market, specific hypotheses to test (if any)
- **Atlas's action**: Design research approach, execute, deliver findings with strategic implications highlighted

### From Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Task assignments with deadlines and priority levels
- **Atlas's action**: Confirm receipt, provide estimated completion time, begin work

## Sends To

### To Maya (Strategy) — Primary
- **What**: Completed research reports, insight summaries, audience profiles, competitive analyses
- **Format**: Structured report with executive summary, numbered findings, and recommendations
- **Expectation**: Maya uses these as the evidence base for strategic decisions

### To Nova (Media)
- **What**: Audience media consumption data, SEO research, keyword strategies, competitive SOV
- **Format**: Data-focused deliverable with tables and actionable recommendations

### To Echo / Finn (Creative)
- **What**: Consumer insight summaries, audience language patterns, competitive creative analysis
- **Format**: Concise brief-style document focused on "what this means for creative"

### To Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Status updates, completion confirmations, blocker notifications
- **Format**: Structured status using Iris's label system

## Handoff Quality Gate
Before any research deliverable leaves Atlas:
1. Does it answer the original research question?
2. Are all findings sourced?
3. Are limitations stated?
4. Is there an executive summary?
5. Are recommendations actionable?
