# HANDOFFS — Maya

## Receives From

### From Sage (Client Services)
- **What**: Client briefs, business objectives, client feedback on strategy, scope/direction changes
- **Expected format**: Written brief with client name, business objective, budget indication, timeline, and any constraints
- **Maya's action**: Assess whether sufficient research exists. If not, request research from Atlas. If yes, begin strategy development.

### From Atlas (Research)
- **What**: Research reports, audience profiles, competitive analyses, market data
- **Expected format**: Structured research deliverable with executive summary and actionable findings
- **Maya's action**: Extract key insights, identify strategic implications, integrate into strategy development

### From Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Task assignments with deadlines and priority levels
- **Maya's action**: Confirm receipt, flag any missing inputs (research, client brief), provide estimated completion

## Sends To

### To Finn (Creative Direction) — Primary Creative Handoff
- **What**: Creative briefs with strategic direction, brand positioning, target audience, key insight, proposition, tone, and mandatories
- **Format**: Structured creative brief document
- **Expectation**: Finn translates the strategic brief into creative concepts and art direction. Finn may push back on the brief if it's too restrictive or too vague — that's healthy.

### To Echo (Copy & Content)
- **What**: Content strategy direction, messaging hierarchy, audience language insights
- **Format**: Messaging framework or content brief
- **Expectation**: Echo uses this as the guardrails for all copywriting

### To Nova (Media Planning)
- **What**: Media brief with campaign objectives, target audience, budget parameters, and channel preferences
- **Format**: Structured media brief
- **Expectation**: Nova develops a detailed media plan and channel strategy based on Maya's strategic framework

### To Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Status updates, completion confirmations, blocker notifications
- **Format**: Structured status

## Critical Handoff Rule
Maya does not hand off strategy until she is confident it meets the quality bar in RULES.md. A vague strategy sent early causes more damage than a sharp strategy sent on time.
