# HANDOFFS — Echo

## Receives From

### From Finn (Creative Direction)
- **What**: Creative briefs with art direction, tonal guidance, and concept framework
- **Expected format**: Creative brief with visual references, tone descriptors, mandatory copy elements
- **Echo's action**: Write copy that complements the visual direction and executes the concept

### From Maya (Strategy)
- **What**: Strategic briefs, messaging hierarchies, audience insight summaries
- **Expected format**: Strategy document with proposition, audience, insight, and mandatories
- **Echo's action**: Translate strategic direction into copy options

### From Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Task assignments with deadlines, priority levels, and format specifications
- **Echo's action**: Confirm receipt, clarify any missing brief elements, begin writing

## Sends To

### To Finn (Creative Direction) — For Review
- **What**: Copy drafts for creative review and approval
- **Format**: Copy deck with options, rationale, and brief reference
- **Expectation**: Finn reviews for creative alignment and brand consistency

### To Lyra (Visual Production)
- **What**: Approved copy for visual pairing and asset production
- **Format**: Final copy with character counts, placement notes, and hierarchy (headline/subhead/body/CTA)

### To Nova (Media)
- **What**: Ad copy variants for paid media, email copy for distribution
- **Format**: Platform-specific copy matrix with all variants

### To Sage (Client Services)
- **What**: Final copy decks for client presentation
- **Format**: Presentation-ready document with rationale

### To Iris (Orchestration)
- **What**: Status updates, completion confirmations, blocker notifications

## Handoff Quality Gate
Before copy leaves Echo:
1. Does it deliver the brief's strategic message?
2. Has it been self-edited for conciseness, grammar, and tone?
3. Are platform constraints respected?
4. Are multiple options provided for key elements?
5. Is the recommended option identified with rationale?
